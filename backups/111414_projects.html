1. Model of actin cable dynamics

Shown below is a 3D java simulation of the actin cable dynamics in fission yeast. Green represents the cytoplasm in the fission yeast cell. The blue spheres are immobile vacuoles that take up a large amount of space in the cell. The yellow sphere in the center stands for the nucleus. Formin protein clusters that localize at the cell tip polymerize actin filaments. These actin filaments are then bundled by cross-linking proteins. Severing proteins such as cofilin are simulated as random filament turnovers. Myosins walk along the actin filament thus generating forces that straighten the actin cables.


This work has been presented at:
1.Biophysical Society 57th Annual Meeting: Sun Feb. 3, 2013 @ Philadelphia, USA (PDF)
2.American Physical Society March Meeting 2013: Wed Mar. 20, 2013 @ Baltimore, USA (PDF)

2. Actin dynamics in droplet

The simulation below shows actin proteins polymerize from random positions and become bundled inside a droplet. The size of the actin network becomes "squeezed" after bundling. Sometimes the network can form a ring structure under certain conditions.

3. Worm-like chain and semiflexible polymers

The single filament below is simulated as semi-flexible polymers. The segment length is 0.1 ?m and the total filament length is 3 ?m. This filament fluctuates under thermal forces with its persistence length set at 10 ?m.

This youtube video shows six filaments are initialized towards one direction and fluctuate through the "zipping" process and finally become bundled. Right panel is tangent correlation showing bundled phase has a much larger persistence length.
