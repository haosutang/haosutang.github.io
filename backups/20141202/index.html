    My major research field of interest is Biophysics. My current research work involves skills including: Physics, Mathematics (esp. PDE), Statistics, Cell biology, Computational simulation and modeling, Programming (Java, Matlab, Mathematica, C++) and Data Analysis.

    My research projects are described in detail here with videos and figures. Projects: 

1.Actin cable dynamics: This is the main research topic towards my PhD thesis. Actin is a protein ubiquitous in living cells. Actin monomers can link to form actin filaments (F-actin). These actin filaments can be bundled together by cross-linking proteins to form actin cables. Here I developed a very comprehensive yet complicated 3D model which includes various protein mechanism to describe the dynamics of actin cables. 

2.Actin dynamics in droplet: Vast amount of research is focused upon the dynamics of actin in vitro. Here I studied the actin dynamics in a confined droplet.

3.Worm-like chain (WLC) and Semiflexible polymers: WLCs are found anywhere in our daily life. They can be as tiny as double-stranded DNA chain which is about 70nm in length (10-8m) to as large as polymeric materials that can span hundreds of meters (103m). I developed a semiflexible polymer model that reflects the WLC behavior and simulated it in Java. I studied its mechanical properties and compared persistence lengths during the process of "zipping" and bundled phases.

4.Lennard-Jones particles: I simulated a system of particles under Lennard-Jones potentials using both molecular dynamics and Monte Carlo algorithm. Constraint conditions were applied to simulate the Lennard-Jones dumbbell systems.

5.Image registration and shape finding: 3D image structures can be generated from stacking 2D image slices. By applying a Gaussian filter and thresholding method it is possible to get rid of some background noise. Then I used region growing to find the shape of the image. I developed a specific shape find method that detects and marks the filamentous shape and was able to extract the skeleton of the shapes and find the mechanical properties accordingly.

6.Quantum information and computation.
